SPORTS FOR ONE, SPORTS FOR ALL IN SKAGIT COUNTY!

Special Olympics was started by Eunice Kennedy Shriver as a way to provide people with intellectual disabilities a place to play and feel included. Through the power of sports and physical activities, people with intellectual disabilities discover new strengths and abilities, skills and success. Our athletes find joy, confidence and fulfillment – on the playing field and in life.

Skagit Special Olympics was established as a community-based program in 1980 to provide local sports training and competitions with a focus on friendships and fun. Athletes have the opportunity to participate in a variety of local Special Olympics Sports offered throughout the year:

  • Bowling, Volleyball, Pickleball (September – November)
  • Basketball (November – February)
  • Cycling, Soccer, Swimming, Bucket Buddies & Walking Club (March – June)
  • Golf (June – August)

Our Mission

To provide year-round sports training and athletic competition in a variety of Olympic-type sports for children and adults with intellectual disabilities, giving them continuing opportunities to develop physical fitness, demonstrate courage, experience joy and participate in a sharing of gifts, skills and friendship with their families, other Special Olympics athletes and the community.

Our Vision

We are a catalyst for inclusion! Special Olympics is the leader in sports, inclusive health, and community building, empowering children and adults with intellectual disabilities to be valued on and off the playing field.

To Register:

In order to participate in Special Olympics, every athlete must be registered into the Special Olympics Portal.
The portal requires the Athlete's:

  • 1: Personal and Emergency contact information
  • 2: Health History
  • 3: Current List of Medications
  • 4: Electronically signed waivers.
In order to create an athlete profile go to: https://portals.specialolympics.org

Please follow one of the guideline documents attached. Choose the instructions that is applicable to the individual completing the portal entry. Either an athlete registering themselves Registration for athletes or in most cases, a guardian or caregiver registering on behalf of the athlete Registration for guardians/caregivers.

VERY IMPORTANT: If using the guidelines for the guardian/caregiver, please note that the first section is asking for the information of the person completing this form. (guardian/caregiver)
Athlete information will be entered at Step 4.

Please make a copy of the entire Athlete Registration Packet for your records (hit the print button) and bring it to the first practice.

Schedules

(Current & Past)

We post and share schedules once facility usage is approved by various organizations (School Districts, Skagit Valley College, Skagit Valley Family YMCA, Parks and Recreation Departments, Avalon Golf Course, Skagit Valley Pickleball). This process takes considerable time and may result in releasing schedules with short notice.
We keep last year’s schedules on line to give athletes, parents and guardians an idea of timelines. We try to stay with the same practice days and times every year.
